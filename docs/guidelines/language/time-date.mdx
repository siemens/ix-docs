# Dates 

## General rules  

Use the ISO 8601 date and time format standards when backend localization is not possible. 

<DoAndDont>
  <DoAndDont.Do>
    <DoAndDont.Item>2025-04-08 (April 8, 2025)</DoAndDont.Item>
	<DoAndDont.Item>14:30:00 (24-hour format)</DoAndDont.Item>
  </DoAndDont.Do>
  <DoAndDont.Dont>
    <DoAndDont.Item>2025/04/08</DoAndDont.Item>
    <DoAndDont.Item>2:30 PM</DoAndDont.Item>
  </DoAndDont.Dont>
</DoAndDont>

Add tooltips or in-line text to support users and clarify which format is being used.  

<DoAndDont>
	<DoAndDont.Do>
		<DoAndDont.Item>YYYY-MM-DD</DoAndDont.Item>
		<DoAndDont.Item>24-hour format</DoAndDont.Item>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Times are formatted in 24-hour format as HH:MM. For example, 2:30 PM is written as 14:30.</DoAndDont.Item>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Dates and times include time zones. For example, April 8, 2025, at 2:30 PM in Berlin is written as 2025-04-08T14:30:00+02:00.</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Do>
</DoAndDont>

Avoid adding unnecessary punctuation to abbreviated dates.  

<DoAndDont>
	<DoAndDont.Do>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Jan</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Do>
	<DoAndDont.Dont>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Jan.</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Dont>
</DoAndDont>

## Dates 

Write out full dates using commas to separate elements within longer texts and paragraphs. 

<DoAndDont>
	<DoAndDont.Do>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Monday, January 12, 2025</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Do>
	<DoAndDont.Dont>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Monday: January 12th: 2025</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Dont>
</DoAndDont>

Write out dates with the abbreviated form using dashes (hyphens) instead of slashes for better readability. 

<DoAndDont>
	<DoAndDont.Do>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Dec-26-2025</DoAndDont.Item>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Apr-09-2025</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Do>
	<DoAndDont.Dont>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Apr/09/2025</DoAndDont.Item>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Apr 09/2025</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Dont>
</DoAndDont>

Write dates in tables using abbreviated months to avoid confusion and the need for further tooltips or explanation. 

<DoAndDont>
	<DoAndDont.Do>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Apr-09</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Do>
	<DoAndDont.Dont>
		<DoAndDont.Item>04-09</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Dont>
</DoAndDont>

Avoid using “st” (as in 1st) or “th” (as in 5th) for a cleaner user interface. 

<DoAndDont>
	<DoAndDont.Do>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Monday, January 12, 2025</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Do>
	<DoAndDont.Dont>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Monday, January 12th, 2025</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Dont>
</DoAndDont>

## Days 

Start all days with a capital letter. Use three-letter abbreviations without punctuation when there isn’t enough space. 

<DoAndDont>
	<DoAndDont.Do>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, etc.</DoAndDont.Item>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Mon</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Do>
	<DoAndDont.Dont>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Mon.</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Dont>
</DoAndDont>

## Weeks 

Avoid calendar weeks (CW) whenever possible as these are very specific to certain regions. 

<DoAndDont>
	<DoAndDont.Do>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Week 15 (April 7 – April 13, 2025)</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Do>
	<DoAndDont.Dont>
		<DoAndDont.Item>CW 15 (15th week of the year)</DoAndDont.Item>
		<DoAndDont.Item>CW 15/2025 (15th calendar week of the year 2025)</DoAndDont.Item>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Calendar week 05 in year 2025</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Dont>
</DoAndDont>

## Months 

Start all months with a capital letter. Use three-letter abbreviations without punctuation when there isn’t enough space.  

<DoAndDont>
	<DoAndDont.Do>
		<DoAndDont.Item>January, February, March, etc.</DoAndDont.Item>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Jan</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Do>
	<DoAndDont.Dont>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Jan.</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Dont>
</DoAndDont>

## Years 

Use the four-digit format and never abbreviate in industrial applications.  

<DoAndDont>
	<DoAndDont.Do>
		<DoAndDont.Item>2023</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Do>
	<DoAndDont.Dont>
		<DoAndDont.Item>’23</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Dont>
</DoAndDont>

## Decades 

Use plural -s when talking about decades.  

<DoAndDont>
	<DoAndDont.Do>
		<DoAndDont.Item>1980s</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Do>
	<DoAndDont.Dont>
		<DoAndDont.Item>1980’s</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Dont>
</DoAndDont>

## Centuries 

Use “th” and “nd” to reference centuries with ordinals without using superscript.  

<DoAndDont>
	<DoAndDont.Do>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Welcome to the 21st century of industrial innovation! Our app uses AI to deliver efficiency in your operations.</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Do>
	<DoAndDont.Dont>
		<DoAndDont.Item>Welcome to the 21st century of industrial innovation! Our app uses AI to deliver efficiency in your operations.</DoAndDont.Item>
	</DoAndDont.Dont>
</DoAndDont>
