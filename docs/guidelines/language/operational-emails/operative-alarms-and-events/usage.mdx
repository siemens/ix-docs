Use these rules alongside the [General rules](../overview-operational-emails/usage.mdx#general-rules). All placeholders are shown with square brackets, e.g. [placeholder]. We have used [App name] as the standard placeholder for you to adapt individually to your product.
:::

## Subject lines

State reason and criticality of the email directly in the subject line.

<div className="dos-and-donts" markdown="true">
<div className="dos" markdown="true">
<ul aria-label="Recommended practices">
<li>[App name] | Failure detected in Plant A</li>
<li>[App name] | Critical risk of cyberattack</li>
<li>[App name] | Threshold change for asset TG_1234</li>

</ul>
</div>
</div>

Use language that reflects the urgency of the email and mentions if actions are required.

<div className="dos-and-donts" markdown="true">
<div className="dos" markdown="true">
<ul aria-label="Recommended practices">
<li>[App name] | Immediate action required | Security vulnerability fix</li>
<li>[App name] | No action required | New events detected</li>

</ul>
</div>
<div className="donts" markdown="true">
<ul aria-label="Practices to avoid">
<li>[App name] | Security issue</li>

</ul>
</div>
</div>

## Body text

Be as specific as possible and tell users exactly what happened (what, when, how).

<div className="dos-and-donts" markdown="true">
<div className="dos" markdown="true">
<ul aria-label="Recommended practices">
<li>Critical alarm immediate attention required: Power supply failure detected in Plant A.<br/>
  &nbsp;&nbsp;&bull; Power supply failure detected in Plant A.<br/>
  &nbsp;&nbsp;&bull; Event details: Overload detected on Transformer T-102 at 14:35 (UTC+2).<br/>
  &nbsp;&nbsp;&bull; Location: Nuremberg facility 32/A.</li>

</ul>
</div>
</div>

Explain consequences and solutions about the event whenever possible.

<div className="dos-and-donts" markdown="true">
<div className="dos" markdown="true">
<ul aria-label="Recommended practices">
<li>What you should do<br/>
Immediate: Reduce the load on T-102 by redistributing power to backup transformers.</li>
<li>Short-term: Inspect the transformer for signs of damage or wear.</li>
<li>Monitor: Check the load distribution system to identify why the overload occurred.</li>
<li>Follow-up: Schedule a maintenance review to prevent future overloads.</li>

</ul>
</div>
</div>

Avoid vague timing or unclear instructions.

<div className="dos-and-donts" markdown="true">
<div className="dos" markdown="true">
<ul aria-label="Recommended practices">
<li>Act immediately.</li>

</ul>
</div>
<div className="donts" markdown="true">
<ul aria-label="Practices to avoid">
<li>Act soon.</li>

</ul>
</div>
</div>

Use clear, action-oriented instructions for users, avoiding words like may, should or recommend.

<div className="dos-and-donts" markdown="true">
<div className="dos" markdown="true">
<ul aria-label="Recommended practices">
<li>Switch to backup power and confirm system status in the portal.</li>

</ul>
</div>
<div className="donts" markdown="true">
<ul aria-label="Practices to avoid">
<li>You should switch to backup power when possible.</li>
<li>You may want to switch to an alternative power supply.</li>

</ul>
</div>
</div>

Explain if and how users can resolve issues or act with links, buttons, and suggestions.

<div className="dos-and-donts" markdown="true">
<div className="dos" markdown="true">
<ul aria-label="Recommended practices">
<li>No action required. The vulnerabilities have been located and logged. [Open Log files] for more details.</li>

</ul>
</div>
<div className="donts" markdown="true">
<ul aria-label="Practices to avoid">
<li>We found 1000 vulnerabilities! This means your plant is in danger!</li>

</ul>
</div>
</div>
