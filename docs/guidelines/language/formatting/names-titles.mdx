# Names and Titles

## General Rules

Use "First name" and "Last name" in forms and avoid "Surname" and "Full name".

<DoAndDont>
  <DoAndDont.Do>
    <DoAndDont.Item>First name</DoAndDont.Item>
    <DoAndDont.Item>Last name</DoAndDont.Item>
  </DoAndDont.Do>
  <DoAndDont.Dont>
    <DoAndDont.Item>Surname</DoAndDont.Item>
    <DoAndDont.Item>Full name</DoAndDont.Item>
  </DoAndDont.Dont>
</DoAndDont>

Allow text fields to accept names with only one letter.

Not all cultures have equivalents to some titles used in the United States, such as Ms.

Use first names to welcome users to applications.

<DoAndDont>
  <DoAndDont.Do>
    <DoAndDont.Item>Welcome, Susanne</DoAndDont.Item>
  </DoAndDont.Do>
</DoAndDont>

## Inclusive Titles

Avoid gendered titles unless absolutely necessary for your application. Use "Title", not "Honorific", to describe words such as Mr. and Mrs.Use non-gendered honorifics in your forms.

<DoAndDont>
  <DoAndDont.Do>
    <DoAndDont.Item>[ Mr. | Ms. | Mx. | None | Other | Prefer not to say ]</DoAndDont.Item>
  </DoAndDont.Do>
  <DoAndDont.Dont>
    <DoAndDont.Item>[ Mr. | Mrs. | Miss | Ms. ]</DoAndDont.Item>
  </DoAndDont.Dont>
</DoAndDont>

## Title Punctuation

Use full stops for all abbreviated titles in American English.

<DoAndDont>
  <DoAndDont.Do>
    <DoAndDont.Item>Mr. Smith</DoAndDont.Item>
  </DoAndDont.Do>
  <DoAndDont.Dont>
    <DoAndDont.Item>Mr Smith</DoAndDont.Item>
  </DoAndDont.Dont>
</DoAndDont>

Use titles with full names or surnames. Avoid using titles when addressing users with only their first name.

<DoAndDont>
  <DoAndDont.Do>
    <DoAndDont.Item>Mrs. Turner</DoAndDont.Item>
    <DoAndDont.Item>Mrs. Jennifer Turner</DoAndDont.Item>
    <DoAndDont.Item>Jennifer</DoAndDont.Item>
  </DoAndDont.Do>
  <DoAndDont.Dont>
    <DoAndDont.Item>Mrs. Jennifer</DoAndDont.Item>
  </DoAndDont.Dont>
</DoAndDont>

Use full stops for all abbreviated professional titles in American English.

<DoAndDont>
  <DoAndDont.Do>
    <DoAndDont.Item>Dr.</DoAndDont.Item>
    <DoAndDont.Item>Prof.</DoAndDont.Item>
    <DoAndDont.Item>Eng.</DoAndDont.Item>
  </DoAndDont.Do>
</DoAndDont>

## Corporate Titles

Use capital letters for corporate titles especially with the name. Use capital letters for corporate title acronyms.Use capital letters for corporate title acronyms.

<DoAndDont>
  <DoAndDont.Do>
    <DoAndDont.Item>Chief Financial Officer Teresa Lopez</DoAndDont.Item>
    <DoAndDont.Item>Teresa Lopez (Chief Financial Officer)</DoAndDont.Item>
    <DoAndDont.Item>Teresa Lopez (CFO)</DoAndDont.Item>
  </DoAndDont.Do>
  <DoAndDont.Dont>
    <DoAndDont.Item>chief financial officer Teresa Lopez</DoAndDont.Item>
  </DoAndDont.Dont>
</DoAndDont>

Use lower case when titles are used generically and without a name. Separate titles from names by placing a comma between them.

<DoAndDont>
  <DoAndDont.Do>
    <DoAndDont.Item>The chief financial officer for the company will hold a press conference tomorrow.</DoAndDont.Item>
    <DoAndDont.Item>The project manager needs to schedule maintenance.</DoAndDont.Item>
    <DoAndDont.Item>Carol Jones, Chairwoman of the Supervisory Board, spoke at the press conference yesterday.</DoAndDont.Item>
  </DoAndDont.Do>
  <DoAndDont.Dont>
    <DoAndDont.Item>Call the Manager.</DoAndDont.Item>
  </DoAndDont.Dont>
</DoAndDont>

Always capitalize Managing Board and use lower case for "member".

<DoAndDont>
  <DoAndDont.Do>
    <DoAndDont.Item>John Smith is a member of the Managing Board.</DoAndDont.Item>
  </DoAndDont.Do>
  <DoAndDont.Dont>
    <DoAndDont.Item>John Smith is a member of the managing board.</DoAndDont.Item>
  </DoAndDont.Dont>
</DoAndDont>
